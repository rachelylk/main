Scaphoid fracture detection

The scaphoid fracture project aims to develop and validate a model to detect scaphoid fractures on x-ray images. The scaphoid bone is a small bone in the wrist, and fractures are difficult to detect: up to 4 in 10 are missed. The project primarily aims to develop a computer vision model to classify x-ray images into fracture, or no fracture, and provide the clinician with a probability of either, and a heatmap of predictive pixels. We are querying the added accuracy of including multi-modal data as predictive variables, such as patient age or sex, or mechanism of injury. We are developing this model using retrospectively collected, anonymised data from Oxford University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust.
